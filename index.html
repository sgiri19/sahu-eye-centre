Patient Education

When should I get a cataract check-up?

If you're experiencing blurred vision, difficulty seeing at night, sensitivity to light, or seeing halos around lights, it's time for a cataract evaluation. We recommend regular eye exams every 1-2 years after age 40, and more frequently if you have diabetes or a family history of eye conditions. Early detection allows for better treatment outcomes.

What are the early signs of glaucoma?

Glaucoma is often called the "silent thief of sight" because it may have no symptoms in early stages. However, warning signs can include gradual loss of peripheral vision, tunnel vision, eye pain, headache, blurred vision, or halos around lights. Regular comprehensive eye exams are crucial, especially if you're over 40, have a family history, or have diabetes.

How often should I have my eyes examined?

Adults should have a comprehensive eye exam every 1-2 years, depending on age and risk factors. Children should be examined at 6 months, 3 years, and before starting school. If you have diabetes, a family history of eye disease, or wear glasses/contacts, more frequent exams may be necessary. Dr. Sahu will recommend a schedule based on your individual needs.

What eye conditions do you treat?

We treat a comprehensive range of eye conditions including cataracts, glaucoma, diabetic retinopathy, macular degeneration, dry eyes, red eyes, refractive errors (nearsightedness, farsightedness, astigmatism), and other common eye problems. We also provide routine eye examinations and vision testing for all ages.

Do you provide spectacles and contact lenses?

Yes, we offer a wide range of premium spectacles and contact lenses at affordable prices. Our optical gallery features stylish frames for all ages, and our team helps you choose the best option for your prescription, lifestyle, and budget. Quality eyewear is essential for clear vision and eye health.
